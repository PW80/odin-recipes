Mac'n'Cheese

Description

Not your typical mac and cheese. Taking inspiration from the classic Ruben - this recipe won 1st place at the Iowa State Fair.

Follow this stripped back guide or visit AllRecipes for the full details.

Ingredients

  • Large Elbow Macaroni
  • Butter
  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Spicy Brown Mustard
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Cayenne Pepper
  • Garlic Granules
  • Whole Milk
  • Gouda
  • Chevre (Goat Cheese)
  • Blue Cheese
  • Corned Beef
  • Sauerkraut
  • Thousand Island Dressing
  • Rye Bread

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook elbow macaroni in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite.
  2. Meanwhile, melt butter in a large skillet. Whisk flour in gradually until smooth. Add mustard, salt, pepper, cayenne, and garlic; stir to combine. Gradually whisk in half-and-half and milk. Cook, whisking continually, until thickened. Add Gouda cheese and stir. Add Chevre and blue cheese and stir until smooth. Remove skillet from heat. Stir cooked macaroni into cheese mixture.
  3. Preheat the oven's broiler.
  4. Divide macaroni and cheese into 6 greased 6-inch cast iron skillets. Top each with corned beef, sauerkraut, a drizzle of Thousand Island dressing, and Gouda and blue cheeses.
  5. Broil skillets in the preheated oven until cheese is golden brown.
  6. Toast rye bread in a toaster and cut each slice diagonally into points. Serve rye bread with Reuben mac.
